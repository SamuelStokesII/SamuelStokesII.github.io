Michael Jackson

Michael Joseph Jackson (August 29, 1958 – June 25, 2009) was an American singer, songwriter, dancer, and cultural icon known worldwide as the “King of Pop.” His unmatched talent, groundbreaking music videos, and revolutionary performances transformed the music industry forever.

Jackson began his career at age 6 as the lead singer of the Jackson 5 and went on to become one of the most influential entertainers in history. His albums Thriller, Bad, and Off the Wall broke racial barriers on MTV and set world records that still stand today. His legacy continues to inspire generations of artists, dancers, and dreamers around the world.
